What is LostWax Casting?

Lost wax casting is a casting process that uses a wax pattern to create a ceramic mold for creating a part or product design. It has been known over the years as lost wax precision casting due to its accuracy in recreating parts with precise tolerances. In modern applications, the lost wax casting is called investment casting. The process was named lost wax casting but is used interchangeably with investment casting.

Technical Notes
Ductile Ni-Resist (Austenitic Ductile Iron)

This metal is often used to replace carbon, stainless steel, and bronze. It offers the following advantages: Because of its high nickel and chromium content, ductile Ni-resist offers More excellent corrosion resistance than carbon and stainless steel, bronze, and equal if not better corrosion resistance than stainless steel and bronze in certain applications.

Ductile Ni-resist is a very stable metal; it isn't affected by extreme changes in ambient or operating temperatures.

Ni-resist produces in our foundry for manufacturing Submersible pumps.
